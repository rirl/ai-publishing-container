== Levinas — Ethical Commandment of the Other

Emmanuel Levinas makes ethics primary: the encounter with another person is the moment when an obligation appears that is radical and asymmetrical.

=== Key ideas

* The “Other” is the other person you meet face-to-face.
* That encounter is experienced as a command: it puts a demand on you before you choose anything.
* The Other’s presence interrupts the self and places responsibility on you.

=== Why it is ethical

* Ethics comes before knowledge or ontology.
* The ethical relation is not based on mutual agreement, rights, or chosen rules.
* It is an immediate responsibility: the Other’s vulnerability calls you to care and protect.

=== What makes it a commandment

* It is “commanding” because it is not optional; it arrives as obligation.
* Unlike Kant’s universal duties, Levinas’ commandment is singular and directed at a particular Other.
* It is not “do if you want”; it is “you are responsible for this Other.”

=== Important features

* Asymmetry: you owe the Other more than the Other owes you.
* Infinity: responsibility never ends; it can never be fully satisfied.
* Face-to-face: the ethical demand is grounded in the face of the Other, which expresses vulnerability and appeals to responsibility.

=== Summary

When you encounter another person, you are immediately called to respond ethically. This call is primary, prior to rules or self-interest, and defines responsibility as the fundamental human relation.
