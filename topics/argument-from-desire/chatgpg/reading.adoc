== Purpose of This Reading List

This curriculum is designed to support rigorous engagement with:

* C.S. Lewis's Argument from Beauty
* Phenomenological critiques of transcendence
* The reconstruction of the argument through Husserl and Stein
* Sartrean existential objections
* Schelerian value realism
* Levinasian ethics of alterity
* Marion’s saturated phenomena
* Contemporary phenomenology of religion

The readings are organized:
1. Foundational
2. Intermediate
3. Advanced/Theological
4. Secondary Scholarship

The order matters.

'''

== Part XII: Final Orientation

The reconstructed Argument from Beauty sits at the intersection of:

* phenomenology,
* ontology,
* ethics,
* aesthetics,
* theology,
* and existential philosophy.

No single thinker resolves all tensions.

The major fault lines remain:

* Husserl vs. Sartre
* Stein vs. Sartre
* Levinas vs. ontology
* Marion vs. metaphysical phenomenology
* transcendence vs. immanence
* givenness vs. constitution
* freedom vs. participation

The reading program above is designed not to eliminate those tensions but to understand them rigorously.

The central question underlying the entire tradition remains:

> Can transcendence genuinely appear within experience without being reduced to subjective projection?

Everything in this curriculum ultimately turns around that question.
